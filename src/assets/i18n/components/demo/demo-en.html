Any questions?

Talks, lectures and since Corona also online seminars usually end with: »Any questions?«. And always nobody answers or the same ones always ask the questions. Hardly anyone in the lecture hall dares to answer, or the questions that one actually wanted to ask during the course of the lecture are forgotten. In the video conferencing system, you are not anonymous. The session may be recorded. The microphones are muted. The chat quickly becomes confusing; the teacher can hardly follow the comments and questions.

If you want your lectures and online seminars to be reflected upon, you need a tool that allows you to ask questions anonymously and silently. Such an open source tool is available free of charge with »frag.jetzt«. Are there any concerns? Is the tool accessible, data protection compliant, immediately usable with any end device without training and without technical understanding? Three times yes.

If I let »frag.jetzt« be used anonymously and thus without social control, do I not invite abuse? No, because the Q&A list can be moderated live by an assistant: Only questions appropriate to the lecture remain on the list. In addition, you can automatically move all new questions to the moderator: They only become public when you or your moderator release them individually.

But I cannot answer all questions in the last ten minutes of my lecture or in the online seminar. Sure. This is where the collective tool aspect comes into play: everyone in the audience can rate each question, in other words, vote up or down. You can answer the questions rated highest by the audience immediately, the others, if you wish, later in »frag.jetzt« or in the course forum of the learning platform.

You can also use the tool before the meeting, to collect questions from the participants and make them available to each other for weighting. This involves the participants even before the meeting, allows you to ask for expectations and previous knowledge and helps you to adapt your presentation or seminar accordingly.

Activate students online and in the lecture hall

Anyone who has held online seminars knows what it means to speak against a »digital wall«: Talking without feedback with a tendency to talk to oneself. You can't tell if the participants are really listening, understanding what they're hearing, or even present at all. Their cameras are turned off. But even when you see the auditorium, for example face-to-face in the lecture hall, there is this uncertainty. Malte Persike explains in a podcast on podcampus.de how to activate students with audience response systems, especially backchannel tools like »frag.jetzt«:

What's so special about »frag.jetzt«?

What makes our backchannel tool different from »Mentimeter«, »Slido«, »Poll Everywhere« and many other commercial feedback apps? It's the result of many years of experience with audience response systems in classroom and online teaching. Besides an intuitive usability, proven in empirical UX studies with hundreds of students, didactic and motivational criteria were decisive for the conception and development of the app, see our ARSnova blog.

Responding to or marking questions

Teachers and moderators can answer questions in the affirmative, in the negative, comment on them, or mark them with a star. Of course, the icons can also be used for other marking purposes. They can also be used as filter criteria to quickly find marked or commented questions. If the teacher has enabled the option, participants can also comment on all questions. Conversations are possible, i.e. comments on comments.

Awarding a bonus for »good questions«

The teacher can mark good questions with a star. The question poser receives an 8-digit code for each star acquired (»token«). The tokens can be redeemed for bonus points by sending an e-mail to the teacher. The teacher checks the submitted tokens against the awarded ones in the bonus archive of their room.

Categorizing questions

The room creator and moderators can define categories (labels) for questions. The questioners can assign their question to one of the given categories, also afterwards. Categorized questions can be filtered by clicking on the label. For example, labeling allows the moderator in a panel discussion to filter questions from the audience by topic or addressee: label »Question to Mr. Biden«, label »Question to Mr. Trump« …

Bookmarking questions

Room creators and participants can individually bookmark questions. The moderators' bookmarks are visible to each other and to the teacher. This allows the moderators to collectively pre-select a session's Q&A session.

Question focus: Presenting questions in full view

For the discussion of the questions on the beamer, they can be presented individually in full view. Advancing to the next or previous question is conveniently done with the arrow keys. You can also activate the display in "autofocus mode": Then, incoming new questions are automatically shown in full view.

On the Q&A list, constantly new questions or ratings can make reading and presenting difficult, as the positions of the questions change. With the pause button the question stream can be frozen. However, new questions or ratings are still possible.

Searching, sorting and filtering questions

The quick location of specific questions is made possible by search, sort and filter options. The full text search also includes the answers. Sorting is possible by time, rating and the degree of controversy. By clicking on the label icon of a question, all questions of this category are displayed. By clicking on the user icon of a question, all questions of this user will be displayed. This allows you to assess the distribution of questions among the question posers: How many people participate? Which questions does one and the same person ask? The following filter criteria can be selected: bonus-awarded, conversations, bookmarked, not bookmarked, answered, unanswered, question number, censored, and own questions.

Identifying controversial questions

To identify controversial questions, that is, questions where the positive and negative votes are balanced and have a value close to zero in the Q&A forum, we introduce an innovative sorting criterion: the degree of controversy or contradiction. First, we form the difference between positive and negative votes and subtract it from the total number of votes. What remains is the absolute number of controversial votes. We divide this value by the total number of votes to get the percentage. Finally, we stretch the percentage with an empirical term so that questions with very few controversial votes are not classified as more controversial than questions with many votes. A simple calculation with great power for controversial question rounds!

Flash Polls

The flash poll lives up to its name: it allows feedback in real time and can be created with just one click. Our feature offers numerous survey templates with single-choice answers such as emoticons, ABCD, school grades, or feedback on the comprehensibility of a presentation or meeting expectations. You can format your questions with Markdown and insert images. Participants will not only see the answer options, but also the question text with images. Display of incoming votes can be enabled to increase engagement. Flash polls encourage participation and formatively provide valuable insights to improve your event.

Quiz rally with »antworte.jetzt«

»frag.jetzt« has a quizzing option: We have included our popular game-based quiz app »arsnova.click«, adapted it a bit and renamed it to »antworte.jetzt«. The info page informs you about its features. The score in the ranking is calculated taking into account the difficulty of the quiz question and the brevity of the answer time. If all quiz questions intended for a bonus are answered correctly, the player receives a bonus code, which can be redeemed by sending an e-mail to the teacher with one click.

Brainstorming

With »frag.jetzt«, brainstorming can be carried out in the spirit of the creativity method. The question is displayed as the heading of a word cloud, so that it is always clear to all participants what they should develop and formulate ideas for. The idea input is anonymous, as with questioning, and can be done as often as desired.

All ideas are displayed in the word cloud synchronously with the input. If only one word is to be entered for an idea, the AI lemmatizes it, i.e. brings it into its basic form, so that multiple entries are reliably recognized and immediately visible due to a larger font.

In the discussion phase of the brainstorming, the ideas can be upgraded or downgraded by all participants by moving the mouse pointer over the entry and clicking on the corresponding icon. The ratings have a direct influence on the size calculation of the font, so that the word cloud updates dynamically. The moderator can edit or ban individual entries. When entering an idea, participants can include their name. The idea providers become visible in the Q&A list if the corresponding filter has been activated.

Mail notification

Registered users can be informed by mail about new questions and answers. Weekday and time of the mail notification can be set.

Free of charge with full functionality

Any number of rooms can be created free of charge as a guest or registered user. The number of participating users in a room is not limited. Only the rooms of registered users remain permanently. However, 180 days after the last use of a room it will be deleted automatically. All questions can be saved with their scores and comments in an Excel spreadsheet.

Accessible

The user interface meets the requirements for readability according to WCAG 2.1 AA. Appropriate display options are available for the beamer presentation and for visually impaired persons. In particular, the font size of the questions can be scaled. In addition to the room code, a room can also be entered via an automatically generated direct link.

Data protection

»frag.jetzt« can be used completely anonymously when registering as a guest. The accounts of registered users are stored and managed in accordance with the data protection regulations of the DSGVO. The program code is »Open Source Software« and can be viewed on GitHub.

Usage options

»frag.jetzt« is operated as free »Software as a Service«, see imprint. If you want to run »frag.jetzt« on your own server, you will find a Docker installation guide on the Project GitLab.

»Progressive Web App« (PWA)

Although an ordinary Web page, it behaves like an app from the app store. You can install it from within the browser without downloading: »Add to Home Page«. After that it will start like an ordinary app. As a PWA, it runs on any smartphone, no matter what operating system, no matter what browser. So your audience will always be ready to use »frag.jetzt« on their mobile devices.

Mobile phone use in the lecture hall

The use of the smartphone during the lecture naturally entails a high risk of distraction. We have learned from our many years of experience with audience response systems in lecture halls, how to didactically approach the risk of distraction:

  1. Mobile phone use during the lecture:

    An assistant moderates the Q&A list on site or from a distance. 10 minutes before the end of the lecture, the teacher answers 2-3 top questions on the beamer. What if I don't have an assistant available? Ask someone from the front row to take over this job. She or he won't say no. Create a moderator guest code for this case.

  2. Mobile phone use only during the break:

    The teacher announces the room code during the break and moderates the questions himself. After the break she answers 2-3 top questions.

  3. No mobile phone use during the lecture (A):

    The teacher creates a new room at the end of each lecture. Students can ask and rate questions at home. The Q&A list is moderated by an assistant or the teacher himself. In the first 10 minutes of the next lecture the teacher answers 2-3 top questions.

  4. No mobile phone use during the lecture (B):

    The teacher creates a room for the entire duration of the course. An assistant moderates the Q&A list during the semester. The teacher marks the relevant questions and deletes all others. The Q&A list thus serves to reflect on the lecture contents and to prepare for the exam.
